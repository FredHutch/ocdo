We are announcing an inaugural data science mentorship program this summer! The Data Science Training Capstone program will let you have an opportunity to work with DaSL instructors Chris Lo or Ted Laderas in a 1:1 mentorship to bring what you learned in our data science classes to a data science project in your professional work! 

Over 6 weeks, you will receive individualized guidance to tackle a data science problem in your professional work that relates to your data science classroom experience with us. We will help you formulate a data science plan in your project, provide starter code, help you troubleshoot, and ultimately you will in charge of writing your own code and interpreting the results. 

If you have taken at least two classes with us by the end of the 2026 Spring Quarter (such as Intro to R/Python/SQL, Intermediate R/Python, Bash for Bioinformatics, Bioconductor for Genomics, Machine Learning for Python), are available between June 15 and July 24, and have a project with a dataset in mind, please fill out this [intake form](https://forms.gle/9nzWTSVWSUhE4gC3A)! 

Due to the pilot nature of this capstone project, we can only accept a small cohort of students this summer, so we may need to prioritize projects in the application process.

Capstone Projects that may be good fit:

- The goal of the project connects directly to what the student learned in the classroom and expands upon it.

- The goal of the project has specific scientific outcomes and/or educational goals.

- The participant has reasons that the dataset can help achieve the goals of the project.

Capstone Projects that may be poor fit:

- The goal of the project is unrelated to what the student learned in the classroom.

- The goal of the project require expertise outside of the student and mentor’s backgrounds.

- The participant is unsure the dataset can help achieve the goals of the project, or the data is not available for use.

What to expect in this application process:
 
1. Fill out the intake form.

2. If a good fit, we will meet up to chat and discuss the project and dataset you have in mind. We will brainstorm together how it can fit into a 6-week capstone project.

3. We will ask you to fill out a capstone project proposal form. 

4. If accepted, we will edit and finalize the scope of the project for approval.
